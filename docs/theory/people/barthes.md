# Roland Barthes

French literary theorist (1915–1980). Moved from structuralism toward post-structuralism.

## Key ideas
- **Myth** — second-order signification; ideology naturalized through sign systems (*Mythologies*)
- **Denotation / Connotation** — first-order vs. second-order meaning
- **Death of the Author** — meaning lives in the reader, not the author's intention
- **Writerly / Readerly texts** — texts that invite interpretation vs. texts that deliver it
- **S/Z** — granular analysis of Balzac's *Sarrasine*; five codes; one of the best examples of narrative decomposition in practice

## Relevance
S/Z is methodologically close to what Plotz does — breaking a text into lexias and tagging them with multiple overlapping codes. The five-code system is worth modeling.
