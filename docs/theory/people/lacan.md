# Jacques Lacan

French psychoanalyst (1901–1981). Reread Freud through Saussurean linguistics.

## Key ideas
- **The unconscious is structured like a language**
- **Metaphor / Metonymy** as the two axes of language (condensation and displacement in Freud)
- **The Symbolic / Imaginary / Real** — three registers of human experience
- **The subject is constituted by language** — there is no pre-linguistic self

## Relevance
Lacan's registers (Symbolic/Imaginary/Real) map loosely onto narrative/history/real-world distinctions in [[narrative]]. The structural claim that the unconscious is linguistic supports treating narrative as a structured formal system.
