# Umberto Eco

Italian semiotician and novelist (1932–2016).

## Key ideas
- **Open / Closed texts** — texts that invite multiple interpretations vs. those that constrain them
- **Model Reader** — the reader implied by the text's interpretive strategy
- **Unlimited semiosis** — sign chains extend indefinitely (links to Peirce)
- **Semiotics of culture** — sign systems are cultural systems
- **Encyclopedic vs. dictionary semantics** — meaning as a network, not a definition

## Relevance
The open/closed text distinction is useful for characterizing corpus choices. Eco's encyclopedic semantics is directly analogous to how Plotz builds meaning through graph connectivity rather than isolated definitions.
