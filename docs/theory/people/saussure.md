# Ferdinand de Saussure

Swiss linguist (1857–1913). Father of modern linguistics and structural semiotics.

## Key ideas
- **Signifier / Signified** — the sign is the arbitrary bond between a sound-image and a concept
- **Arbitrary nature of the sign** — no natural connection between word and meaning
- **Langue / Parole** — the system vs. the individual utterance
- **Synchronic vs. diachronic** — analysis of a system at one moment vs. over time
- **Difference** — meaning arises from contrast, not from inherent properties

## Relevance
The signifier/signified split is foundational to any structured representation of text. Plotz works in the space of signifiers; the ontology layer is where signifieds get modeled.
